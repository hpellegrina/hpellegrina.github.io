Working Papers

Scaling Up Agricultural Insurance
with Jeremy Majerovitz  ·  May 2026
Accepted for presentation at JME (CRNYU Series)
Should governments subsidize rainfall insurance for farmers in developing countries? We study this question using a general equilibrium model calibrated to Ghana, bringing experimental moments from previous studies to discipline the structural parameters. The optimal subsidy trades off a fiscal externality, a pecuniary externality from crop-price changes, and a fiscal incidence term — and is close to zero.
Public R&D Meets Economic Development: Embrapa and Brazil's Agricultural Revolution
with Ariel Akerman, Jacob Moscona, and Karthik Sastry  ·  Mar. 2026
R&R, American Economic Review VoxEU NBER Digest
Can public R&D investments in developing countries drive productivity growth? We study this question in the context of Brazilian agriculture and the Empresa Brasileira de Pesquisa Agropecuária (Embrapa), a public research corporation established in 1973 to develop locally suitable science and technology. We study the effects of Embrapa on the direction of agricultural research and on agricultural productivity. Structural results indicate substantial agricultural productivity gains, implying large returns to R&D, much of which is explained by Embrapa's dispersed network of centers.
Farm Size Distribution, Weather Shocks, and Agricultural Productivity
with Julian Arteaga, Margarita Gáfaro, Ana María Ibáñez, and Nicolás de Roux  ·  Jun. 2026
Submitted VoxDev
We document new empirical facts about farm size dynamics and the impact of weather shocks across developing countries. Using an administrative dataset for Colombia, we also show that weather shocks increase the frequency of land sales and reduce farm sizes within municipalities. We calibrate a dynamic, heterogeneous household model in which uninsured farmers make landholding and occupational choices. We find that temporary weather shocks have long and persistent effects on the economy, taking more than a decade to fade out.
Deforestation: A Global and Dynamic Perspective
with Farid Farrokhi, Elliot Kang, and Sebastian Sotelo  ·  Dec. 2025
R&R, American Economic Review TradeTalks
We study the impacts of trade costs on deforestation through a dynamic, global perspective. We build a dynamic GE model of trade and land use, in which structural change and comparative advantage determine the extent, location, and timing of deforestation. Results indicate that the global forest area would expand in response to multilateral trade liberalization in agriculture, but that it would shrink when the liberalization is experienced by individual regions.
